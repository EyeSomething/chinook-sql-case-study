# Summary of Findings

## A. Understanding the Data
the dataset includes at least these tables:
Album
Artist
Customer
Employee
Genre
Invoice
InvoiceLine
MediaType
Playlist
PlaylistTrack
Track

### Customer Table
#### Columns:
CustomerId, FirstName, LastName, Company, Address, City, State, Country, PostalCode, Phone, Fax, Email, SupportRepId

#### Findings:
Customers are spread across multiple countries (Brazil, Germany, Canada, Denmark, Czech Republic, etc.)
Some customers have a Company, many do not (typical for individual consumers).
SupportRepId links each customer to an Employee in Customer Support.

### Invoice Table
#### Columns:
InvoiceId, CustomerId, InvoiceDate, BillingAddress, BillingCity, BillingState, BillingCountry, BillingPostalCode, Total

#### Findings:
Each row is a single purchase.
The Total column gives revenue per order.
Invoices are tied to customers via CustomerId.
Billing country is important for regional sales analysis.

### InvoiceLine Table
#### Columns:
InvoiceLineId, InvoiceId, TrackId, UnitPrice, Quantity

#### Findings:
This is a line-item table, meaning every track purchased is listed here.
Connects invoices (sales) with tracks (products).
Quantity is usually 1 for digital tracks, but it can differ.
UnitPrice matches track price.

### Track Table
#### Columns:
TrackId, Name, AlbumId, MediaTypeId, GenreId, Composer, Milliseconds, Bytes, UnitPrice

#### Findings:
Contains all track metadata.
MediaTypeId and GenreId link to their respective tables.
Milliseconds = track duration
Price appears consistent (mostly 0.99).
